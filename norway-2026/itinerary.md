**Group:** 5–6 guys, ~23 yo, fit (gym/running) but not hardcore backpackers
**Arrive:** Tue Aug 25 dep → **Wed Aug 26, land Oslo (OSL) 12:35 PM**
**Depart:** **Mon Sep 7, fly out of Oslo 1:50 PM**

---

## The big picture (and *why* it's shaped this way)

The single most important logistics fact, verified against Innlandstrafikk's official 2026 timetables:

> **All the cheap seasonal "feeder" buses into Jotunheimen STOP on Aug 16, 2026** — the Otta→Gjendesheim local bus, the Lom→Spiterstulen shuttle, and the Lom→Juvasshytta shuttle are all done for the season before you arrive.

What this means in practice:
- The **one** public route that still runs in your window is the **direct Valdresekspressen coach Oslo ↔ Gjendesheim** (NOR-WAY, runs into early October).
- So the smart, cheap, no-car move is: **bus in to Gjendesheim, hike a loop, bus out from Gjendesheim.** One coach each way + a ferry. **No rental car, no taxis, no transfer-juggling.** This is exactly your stated priority (convenience + money).
- A point-to-point traverse ending at Spiterstulen/Galdhøpiggen would **strand you** (no September transport out of the mountains except a chartered private minibus to Lom). So we **loop back to Gjendesheim** instead — and we still bag Galdhøpiggen as a day-trip from Spiterstulen along the way.

**Structure:** Oslo (recover/prep) → 7-day Jotunheimen loop → Oslo (city day) → fly.

| Phase | Dates | Nights |
|---|---|---|
| Oslo — arrival + prep | Aug 26–27 | 2 |
| **Jotunheimen loop** | **Aug 28 – Sep 5** | **8 (mountain)** |
| Oslo — city day | Sep 5–6 | 2 |
| Fly home | Sep 7 | — |

That's **~65/35 mountain/city** — your target. Two nights in Oslo up front (jetlag recovery + a fuel/gear/grocery prep day), **7 hiking days**, then two nights back in Oslo at the end (arrival evening + one full city day), all of it car-free. The extra mountain day vs. a city-heavy version is spent making the route *gentler*, not longer-per-day: the old ~11 mi (~18 km) Leirvassbu→Gjendebu slog is now split into two moderate legs via Olavsbu.

> **A note on units:** distances and elevations below lead with **miles/feet**, with metric in parentheses — because every Norwegian trail sign, DNT map, ferry/bus schedule, and weather forecast (yr.no) you'll actually use out there is in **km, meters, and °C**. So the parentheses are the numbers you'll match against signage on the ground.

# PART 2 — JOTUNHEIMEN LOOP: Aug 28 – Sep 5

**Shape:** Gjendesheim → Besseggen ridge → Memurubu → Glitterheim → Spiterstulen (+ Galdhøpiggen) → Leirvassbu → Olavsbu → Gjendebu → **ferry** back to Gjendesheim → coach to Oslo. *(One continuous traverse — the only boat is the scenic finale across Gjende.)*
**7 hiking days**, ~62 mi (~100 km) on foot + Norway's highest peak, built around **2–3 "push" days early and a gentle, tapering back half**, two of the marquee days carried on a **day-pack** (Besseggen + Galdhøpiggen). The arc front-loads the epics while you're fresh, then eases out over three moderate days to the exit.

### Route at a glance
| Day | Route | Dist | Time | Effort | Pack | The payoff |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **D1** Aug 29 | Gjendesheim → **Besseggen** ridge → Memurubu | ~8.5 mi (14 km) | 6–7 h | Hard (grade-1 scramble) | Full | The Gjende/Bessvatnet view — the iconic one |
| **D2** Aug 30 | Memurubu → **Glitterheim** | ~12 mi (19 km) | ~7 h | Push (long) | Full | Russvatnet, remote high country |
| **D3** Aug 31 | Glitterheim → **Spiterstulen** (low Skautflye route) | ~10 mi (16 km) | ~5.5 h | Moderate | Full | Into the high-peaks heart, Visdalen |
| **D4** Sep 1 | **Galdhøpiggen** 8,100 ft (2,469 m), out-and-back from Spiterstulen | ~7.5–8.5 mi (12–14 km) | 7–9 h | Strenuous (4,850 ft climb) | **Day pack** | **Roof of Norway. No guide/glacier needed.** |
| **D5** Sep 2 | Spiterstulen → **Leirvassbu** | ~9 mi (15 km) | ~5 h | **Recovery** (gentle) | Full | Mellow Visdalen valley, 6,500 ft peaks |
| **D6** Sep 3 | Leirvassbu → **Olavsbu** area | ~7–9 mi (11–15 km) | 4–5 h | Moderate | Full | **Kyrkja (6,665 ft)**, Rauddalsbandet, remote wild camp in Rauddalen |
| **D7** Sep 4 | Olavsbu → **Gjendebu** | ~7–9 mi (11–15 km) | 4–5 h | Moderate | Full | Rauddalen, back down to Lake Gjende |
| **D8** Sep 5 | Ferry Gjendebu → Gjendesheim → **coach to Oslo** | — | travel | easy | Full | — |

> **Two sections to respect** (both fine for fit, careful hikers in dry weather): the **Besseggen scramble** (D1) — a ~1,300 ft hands-on grade-1 section; through-hiking E→W you **descend** it into Memurubu with full packs (the committing direction — take it slow and spot each other). Anyone uneasy can take the lakeside **Bessvatnet bypass** (+2.5 mi, no scramble). And **none of the sketchy Bukkelægret descent** — we deliberately ferry out from Gjendebu instead of hiking that chained section.

## Conditions & safety (late Aug–early Sep, ~61.5°N)
- **Daylight is plentiful** (~14 h on Sep 1, sunrise ~06:15 / sunset ~20:40) but shrinking ~5 min/day — **start early, carry headlamps.**
- **Temps:** valleys 45–52 °F high / 34–39 °F low (7–11 °C / 1–4 °C); on the high passes and Galdhøpiggen subtract ~10–18 °F — **expect frost up high and possible near-freezing daytime in bad weather.** Pack a real shell + insulation + gloves/hat.
- **~44% chance of rain on any day.** Plan for wet roughly every other day.
- **Snow can fall above ~4,900 ft (1,500 m) anytime** and hides cairns on Besseggen/high passes. **Don't start exposed sections (Besseggen, Galdhøpiggen) in fog or strong wind.**
- **Camping every night = committing to cold, possibly wet pitches this late.** Bring a **3-season tent that handles wind**, a sleeping bag rated to ~**25 °F / -4 °C** (or warmer), and an **insulated pad (R-value ≥ 3.5)**. This is the gear that makes or breaks the trip.
- **The huts are your safety margin** — staffed and open through ~Sep 13, so even though you're tenting there's a warm bailout + kiosk resupply the whole route. If a real storm rolls in, duck into one (you've got the key). Summer footbridges are still in.
- Follow the **Fjellvettreglene** (Norwegian Mountain Code); leave a route plan with someone and check trailhead/hut info boards; *"it's never too late to turn around."*

## Dialing the split / alternatives

**Want even more mountain (push past 65/35)?** Skip the Aug 27 Oslo prep day: buy fuel/food the afternoon you land (Aug 26), coach to Gjendesheim **Aug 27**, and add an 8th hiking day (e.g. an extra night at Glitterheim with a Glittertind 8,090 ft / 2,465 m summit, or a Memurubu night). Costs you the jetlag buffer, and you'd only have the Aug 26 arrival evening in Oslo at the start.

**Want it gentler / cheaper (skip the big summit):** Drop the Glitterheim–Spiterstulen–Galdhøpiggen northern arm and do a relaxed **western Gjende loop** instead — Besseggen, then ferry to Gjendebu and circuit **Gjendebu ↔ Olavsbu ↔ Leirvassbu** (self-service Olavsbu needs the DNT key, which your membership covers), with 5–8 mi (8–13 km) days and lots of camping flexibility. More chill, no 4,850 ft climb, but you trade away Norway's highest peak.

**Set on the glacier-guide Galdhøpiggen experience?** It exists from **Juvasshytta** (10:00 daily departures through Sep 13, ~490 NOK pp, crosses the Storjuvbreen glacier roped-up). But it forces a messier route + a private transfer out of the mountains in September. The **Spiterstulen non-glacier route in this plan gets you the same summit, free, with clean logistics** — recommended unless the roped-glacier crossing is specifically the experience you want.
